IISDG

The upcoming 1st International Conference on Innovation and Implementation of Sustainable Development Goals (ICoIISDG) will be held on 23rd – 25th December 2019 in at Bhopal, India. The conference is hosted by MPCST in collaboration with NIDAAN and SHEFWELL.

The aims of the 1st ICoIISDG is to provide an international forum for exchanges of knowledge and dissemination of information on planning theories and practices with focus on India accomplishment in the attempt to support sustainable development goals in the region.

The forum is expected to build a platform in urban and regional planning within the broad framework of sustainable development within Southeast Asia context in particular and Asia in general. The conference also seeks to develop international collaboration and network of academics, professionals, practitioners, policy makers and students who share same interests and concerns on the achievement of sustainable development goals through their respective roles and interests.

The conference invites academics, professionals, practitioners, policy makers, students, non-government organizations, community-based organizations, and those associated with urban and regional planning to present and share their result results, experiences, views and opinions.

As reflected in the title of this conference, the conference theme is Sustainable Communities in Urban and Regional Planning. The theme was inspired by willingness of the planning communities to support the Sustainable Development Goals set by the United Nations. The planning communities, to some extent, will be able to take part on the accomplishment of the Goals, through whatever the planning communities can do and contribute, regardless the scale of impacts. For the past decades we have been developing our knowledge and practice on how to achieve sustainability. We have also been experiencing in coping with challenges on sustainability matters in our life. Thus, we have sufficient practical knowledge and experience in facing the present and future challenges on sustainability. If we are referring to the agreed definition of sustainable development, which has been introduced for more than three decades ago, as it is well documented in “Our Common Future”. Sustainability is defined as the ability to maintain our capacity to meet the needs of current generation without compromising the ability to provide the needs of future generation. During the very first years of the course, lots of pros and cons and debates on this sustainability issue were particularly on to which extent the development would be allowed, which is known as the term of brown sustainability, and the magnitude of the natural resources utilization, which is also termed as green sustainability. Brown sustainability is mainly the concern of under-developed and developing countries, on the other hand the green sustainability is of the concerns of most developed countries. In this fast developing technology era towards Society 5.0, we need to revisit the current state of our paradigm and communities in the developing countries and to realign our current concepts and practices of sustainability in order to be able to re-calibrate our plan towards sustainable development. Academics, professionals, practitioners, policy makers, NGOs, CBOs and students will play their important roles in accomplishing sustainable development through their respective planning roles. This conference attempts to provide a platform for this purpose.

    Conference Tracks

    To cater the wider views, voices, concerns and opinion, the conference sets various sub-themes which are expected to embrace a very comprehensive knowledge on urban and regional planning. The themes are as the followings. SUSTAINABLE COMMUNITY PLANNING. Communities are the primary concern of the planning theory and practices. The community is a subject and also object of the planning process, and sustainable community is one of the objectives of a city plan or rural plan. It is indeed difficult to do a community planning as community is a dynamic entity particularly if we refer to future Society 5.0, at the same time a planning refers to a particular time horizon. This two diametrical situation is a unique challenge for planners. Findings, views, and opinions on this issue will enrich the horizon towards Society 5.0 within the domain of community. BALANCING RURAL-URBAN DIGITAL DIVIDES TOWARDS SUSTAINABILITY. In the Society 5.0, big data stems from physical space would be processed by artificial intelligence for the comfort of mankind. In the meantime, in the current society, there is a large gap on the understanding of the digital world between urban and rural society. This situation certainly deters the sustainability in urban and rural society. Closing the gaps of digital knowledge and use on urban and rural society is a robust challenge. Presentations on this matter will be highly appreciated. CURRENT AND APPLIED TECHNOLOGY IN PLANNING FOR SUSTAINABLE COMMUNITIES. The leap-frog into Society 5.0 will need the use of technology. Does present technology enable to respond to current challenges and ready to welcome Society 5.0? Discussion and presentation and current technology and applied technology in planning to accomplish current sustainable development goals, or future technology in planning towards Society 5.0 will be most welcome. Again, this sub-theme puts community as the goal. SUSTAINBLE RURAL-URBAN ECONOMICS. This traditional knowledge of rural and urban economics are still important amid the change in society, with a ground that change in society will not be taken place at once rather gradual in nature. The discussion and presentation on sustainable rural-urban economics are expected to encompass the issue on how rural community promote their wellbeing through a sustainable manner i.e. minimizing the dilemma of environmental protection and rural resource uses. On the other hand, urban economics should focus on third stage of development economics i.e. service-oriented economic and left resource-based or agriculture-based economics behind. SOCIAL INCLUSION IN PLANNING. Society is traditionally the object of a plan. Amid the object of the plan, the community at large is commonly excluded in the decision making process in planning, particularly of the top-down planning is applied. Discussion and presentation on theory and/or experience in implementing bottom-up planning and top-down planning with respect to monitoring process, results of the planning, acceptance by community on the programs implemented as a result of a plan is most welcomed. SUSTAINABLE HUMAN SETTLEMENTS. Human settlements, particularly in developing countries, are still a persistent problem. Backlog as a result of disparity between shortfall supply and high demand on housing is ubiquitous in developing countries, and becomes a substantial problem in most Asian countries. It is understood if the central or federal government places a special program on low-cost housings, in both rural areas and cities. Presentation on the current status and challenges on the provision of housing in a sustainable way is expected. URBAN ENVIRONMENTALISM FOR SUSTAINABILITY. Environmental is one of the three aspect of conventional sustainability. Urban environmentalism aims at compromising the use of urban resources in sustainable ways for the welfare of citizens without at all sacrificing the sustainability of the resources. Ecological modernization is a popular way to accomplish urban environmentalism. Presentation on this matter on theories or experiences is expected. URBAN HERITAGE. Cities are developed from the long history. Therefore, the existence of historical buildings and artifacts cannot be separated from the city history. The discussion on the concepts and theory as well as experience in conserving urban heritage, or promoting urban heritage at the next level of urban tourism is expected. Case studies on prominent cities in conserving the urban heritage, or challenges in safeguarding and sustaining urban heritages are most welcomed. SUSTAINABLE URBAN MOBILITY. Mobility of urban citizens is paramount. The city is alive when the mobility exists in a dynamic and comfort manner. In a planning theory, urban mobility comes at the primary variables after space and land uses. Thus, when discussing a city or any urban areas, it is incomplete without discussing urban mobility. However, this conference attempts to address not just mobility per se, but sustainable urban mobility, where consideration on the sustainability is given emphasis EMERGING TECHNOLOGY IN URBAN AND REGIONAL PLANNING. Implementation of technology in urban and regional planning can be a great benefit. Technology could enhance community engagement, increase efficiency of planning process, strengthen data accuracy, and extend the range of data collection and utilization. Variation of examples regarding how do and what kind of innovation of technology for-dealing with urban and regional planning problems and to what extent those implementation are potential to increase the quality of planning process and evaluation are substantial to be discussed particularly in the digital era. This is even more crucial in the context of the Global South where urban and regional planning is facing an extremely rapid urbanization, which means more efficient, inclusive, accurate and collaborative way of urban and regional planning is needed. In this context, the role of technology is essential. Paper presentation regarding this matter is welcome.

Submission Guidelines

Submission Guidelines

Papers may be submitted at here in accordance with the following guidelines.

  • Oral Paper Submission Guidelines:

    1. All the submissions should be in MS word document, font type- Times New Roman, font size-12 point, and double spaced.

    2. The references should be in the latest Springer (APS) style sheet.

    3. All submissions should be anonymous to facilitate a double-blind peer review, and should contain an abstract (250-300 words) and keywords (4-5).

    4. The full paper is expected to be between 4000-6000 words, including all references and other items.

    5. The paper should contain the following headings: Introduction; Review of Literature; Methodology; Results & Discussion (Findings) and Conclusion

    6. The paper should have sections and headings such as 1, 1.1, 1.2, 2… and so on.

    7. While submission, the author(s) may indicate if they would want to make a poster presentation if their paper is not accepted for oral presentation but can be accepted for poster presentation.

  • Poster Presentation Guidelines

    The participants willing to make poster presentations may indicate the same while making their submissions. They have a choice of structuring their submission(s) as per the oral paper submission guidelines as above or as per the poster display guidelines as below. However, upon acceptance as poster presentation, they need to structure the posters as per the following guidelines.

    1. A poster should be as self-explanatory as possible. It should be clear, structured, concise, and attractive.

    2. The proposal for a poster presentation should consist of an abstract (300 words at maximum) and an extended summary (1000 words at maximum, including references).

    3. Poster format should include the following headings: Introduction; Objectives; Material & Methods; Results & Discussion, and Conclusion.

    4. Poster Title: 30-35 font size in Arial Black – Body Text: 27-30 font size in Arial with single spacing.

    5. The images (clips arts, graphs, diagrams, and drawings) must be in high resolution EPS format (300 dpi).

    6. Poster Orientation: Portrait format

    7. Poster Materials: Paper or cardboard and should be laminated.

    8. Dimensions of the poster boards are 90cm (height) x 70cm (width), submitted as one piece.

About Bhopal

Bhopal is the capital city of the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh and the administrative headquarters of Bhopal district and Bhopal division. The city was the capital of the former Bhopal State. Bhopal is known as the City of Lakes for its various natural as well as artificial lakes and is also one of the greenest cities in India. It is the 17th largest city in the country and 131st in the world. Bhopal houses various educational and research institutions and installations of national importance, including ISRO's Master Control Facility. BHEL, and AMPRI. Bhopal is home to the largest number of Institutes of National Importance in India, namely IISER, MANIT, SPA, AIIMS, NLIU and IIIT.
